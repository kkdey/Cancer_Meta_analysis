We now fit a Random effects meta-analysis model. We use two Random effects methods- the Mantel Haenszel or the DerSimonian-Laird (DL) method, which is the most commonly followed method in meta analysis and the REML (Restricted Maximum Likelihood) method, which has shown by Viechtbauer’s simulation study (2002) to be most optimal of the random effect meta analysis methods.

Now we focus on the tests of heterogeneity, the values f \(Q\) have been reported above for both the Random effects methods and the Fixed effect method,however \(Q\) statistic in this case is not recommended as a measure of heterogeneity bacause the number of trials is small, only 4, and therefore the Q test will have lower power. So, we consider other measures of heterogeneity
